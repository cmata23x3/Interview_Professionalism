Before the Interview Setting Up Success

Before the interview, there are some preliminary items that need to be completed, such as writing a resume and cover letter. Here are some steps for making a good first impression and getting your foot in the door.

Weeks Before

Writing a Resume The First Step to Interviewing

The beginning of your path for interviewing is writing a resume, a straight-forward, single page snapshot of your education, experiences, skills and achievements.

  • Don't make your resume too long.
    • If you're not a Ph.D. candidate, choose the most important information and keep to one page.
  • When writing your work experience, give short desciption below it that entails some of the tasks you undertook.
    • Showcase what you've worked on and done and always lead with a action verb.
    • For example, "Produced an Excel document to manage store inventory"
  • Do use technical buzzwords.
    • "Python", "Java", etc. These words help when employers are searching through resumes.
  • Try and avoid adjectives.
    • For example, "Prestigious awards." Don't overwhelm the employer with grandiose terms.
  • Be prepared by obvious weaknesses that you may have in your resume.
    • Although you may not like to think so, if something is left off your resume, be prepared to possibly discuss it during the actual interview.
    • A common example would be if your Grade Point Average is a bit below average and you decide to leave it off, don't be caught off guard if the interviewer asks about it.

Day of

Pre-Interview Preparation Steps to Get Ready for the Interview

It's the day of your interview. You've gotten your foot in the door and now to get prepared for your interview.

  • Rule of Thumb: Never dress less than the attaire that you'd expect your interviewer to be wearing.
    • If you're interviewing at a Fortune 500 company and you expect your interviewer to be wearing a full suit, then suit up as well.
    • If you think it may be a bit more casual, a dress shirt and slacks will do, but never should the interviewer be in a more formal attaire than you.
  • Be punctual and courteous.
    • It's never a good start when you're late to your interview, so plan ahead and make sure you get there with time to spare.
    • When finally meeting your interviewer, be polite, speak clearly, give a firm handshake, and always look them in the eye when speaking. These are a few things that will give you an air of confidence.
